How I like to buy a car

January 29, 2014 trajano Leave a comment

Recently I leased two cars for my family’s use. I would summarize my experience with five different dealerships.

Markham Subaru
Scarborough Subaru
Don Valley Toyota
Richmond Hill BMW
East Court Ford Lincoln

The gist is this.
When I start I am not sure what to get yet. So demonstrate the cars to me.

I usually indicate my pain points namely parking. Don’t dismiss my pain points and say just get practice.

When I have an idea what I want I want to be able to try similar cars. Don’t make it too difficult for me to test drive other cars.

I want the best deal possible without the act of going to manager for approval. If you can’t give me a good rate and need approval then send me directly to the one who can rather than waste my time.

I want all the possible charges laid out in front and show me the final number rather than surprise more fees.

Also you tell me the price and we will work from there. Not the other way around.

The experience

Richmond Hill BMW, Scarborough Subaru and Markham Subaru did just that at least most of it. Markham Subaru won the SUV sale just because the location was more convenient and because the Subaru Forrester was the better car. Though the sales experience had a few minor glitches in the experience. Scarborough Subaru had the best experience just the location wasn’t as good as the Markham one.

Now for my own car which had to be a sedan wasn’t as smooth an experience. Toyota did the dance around with the sales and manager that it was such a turn off and the Prius was a terrible car the year I was test driving. Ford was the same but they had a better car that just said BUY ME. So in the end Ford won the sale for my car but because of the car rather than the sales experience. Also I stuck with that dealership because I have been with their service center for over 13 years with my last car and I like to keep the relationship.

Got the Hangout update finally

November 16, 2013 trajano Leave a comment

These rolling updates are quite annoying. However, I finally got the fabled Hangout update that supports SMS.

My only other comparison is the MightyText app and iMessage.

Anyway the SMS addition to hang out is a welcome change. However it still lacks a few usability features.

First of all, you simply just have another SMS client, the only thing is you can see chat like messages all in one spot.

However, they are still treated like separate contacts rather than a unified contact view.

Try it out when you SMS a person who is on your hangout you do not see message logs that are unified, it is a different message log per method of contact.

It also does not sync up the messages with Hangout on the tablet. Unlike the MightyText app which does that job.

For now, I have uninstalled MightyText to see what else I could be missing.

In the iOS world

I recently got my first iPhone and I verified that iMessage can send SMS and chats at the same time (or at least give the illusion of it), but only on the Mac and the iPhone. There is no PC version.

Still, Hangouts a good start but also very disappointing.

Don’t use hosting24

August 20, 2013 trajano Leave a comment

I am in the process of moving away from 000webhost whose parent company is hosting24. Though I expected to do it later than this soon.

Seems like they hold your data hostage, as I expected, and I did put a bit of contingencies at least with most of the content, but the database was a bit of an issue because there was no direct access to the database to do periodic backups and I wasn’t able to put enough time to manage the staged deployment of content to a WordPress system hosted on 000webhost.

Anyway, for those who are cheap I guess 000webhost is still an okay choice provided you don’t use any facility aside from HTML hosting like for Maven sites.Â  However, I won’t recommend them for anything of use.

I won’t recommend their parent company, hosting24, just because they support such an unscrupulous practice to lure people in.Â  Instead I would likely refer people to either GoDaddy which already does the DNS or Dave’s Network which my friend uses for hosting in Canada at a relatively cheap rate.

In fact, I may switch to Dave’s Network if my website requires a bit more uptime to drive business, but as this website is still primarily a hobby for me I can’t justify paying more than $0.

For now it is going to take a couple of weeks before I totally restore the web site again.Â  However, this time I have better control over how things work.
